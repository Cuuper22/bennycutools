Understanding Your TPMS
What That Light Means

The TPMS light appears when any tire drops 25% below recommended pressure. In Cherokee County's temperature swings, a 20¬∞F change equals ~2 PSI change.

Georgia Summer Tip

Check pressure in the morning before driving. Hot asphalt can raise readings 4-6 PSI, masking a low tire.

When to Worry

Don't panic: Small drops (2-3 PSI) on cold mornings are normal.
Take action: Consistent drops of 5+ PSI, or one tire losing pressure faster than others.

Rotation Schedule
Maximize your tire investment
Every 5,000-7,500 Miles

Regular rotation ensures even wear across all four tires. Front tires typically wear faster due to steering and braking forces.

Alignment Matters

Cherokee County roads can take a toll on alignment. Here's when to check:

  • After hitting a significant pothole
  • Every 12,000-15,000 miles
  • When installing new tires
  • If steering pulls to one side
  • Uneven tire wear visible
