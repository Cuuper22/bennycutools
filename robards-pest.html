Seasonal Pest Calendar

January

Overwintering pests (rodents, lady beetles, stink bugs) seeking warmth indoors.

February

Similar to January, rodents remain active. Early signs of termites may appear.

March

Termite swarm season begins. Ants become more active as temperatures rise.

April

Peak termite activity. Spiders, roaches, and mosquitoes start to emerge.

May

Mosquitoes, ticks, and fleas become problematic. Ants and roaches continue to thrive.

June

Stinging insects (wasps, hornets) are active. Spiders, roaches, and mosquitoes at their peak.

July

Continued high activity for mosquitoes, roaches, ants, and stinging insects.

August

Summer pests remain strong. Prepare for fall invaders seeking shelter.

September

Spiders and rodents start moving indoors. Cooler weather brings relief from some insects.

October

Rodent proofing is crucial. Spiders and overwintering pests seek shelter.

November

Rodents and overwintering pests (stink bugs, lady beetles) indoors for winter.

December

Continued rodent activity. Focus on interior pest management and prevention.

Pest ID Guide

Identify common pests in the Clarksville area with our visual guide.

Ants

Small, segmented body. Common in kitchens, seeking food and water. Can build large colonies.

Spiders

Eight legs, two body segments. Often found in quiet, undisturbed areas. Most are harmless.

Cockroaches

Flat, oval bodies. Nocturnal, thrive in warm, moist environments. Can spread diseases.

Mosquitoes

Small, flying insects known for their bites. Breed in standing water and can transmit diseases.

Rodents (Mice/Rats)

Gnawing mammals. Seek shelter and food indoors, especially in colder months. Can cause damage and spread disease.

Termites

Social insects that feed on wood. Can cause significant structural damage to homes if left unchecked.
